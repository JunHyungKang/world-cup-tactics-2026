# Product Thesis

Product selection ID: `corner-war-room`

Product data scope: `official-open-historical-tactics`

## Winning goal

Build the entry judges remember as **the evidence-first set-piece war room**:
the judge moves one outlet role into a corner-defending duty, then recorded World
Cup evidence shows both a scene that touches that promise and a shot scene the
promise does not explain.

This makes the creator's strongest capabilities felt through the product rather
than announced as an AI feature: adversarial evaluation instead of agreeable
recommendations, immutable source evidence, reproducible transformations,
deterministic domain logic, and fail-closed output. `Scout / Coach / Skeptic` are
secondary views of that workflow, not the protagonist or runtime AI agents.

> Do not ask AI for the best formation. Put your corner plan on trial against
> what the opponent actually did.

## Gate result

`PASS — implementation gate` for **Corner War Room / 세트피스 재판실**.

The official DAKER Data-tab rule permits any year/tournament/player composition,
so historical-data scope is not conditional on a board answer. The CC BY 4.0
World Cup 2018 Events and Matches sources, all 42 Brazil corner windows, the
deterministic transform, corrected boundary sensitivity, and 42/42 independent
structural/semantic audit have passed the data and football gates. The selected
interaction now replaces the former Touchline Lab shell; no synthetic score is
shown as football advice.

The 2026-07-17 source spike verified the historical dataset and found 603 corners.
Corner War Room uses 2018 only as a labeled historical evidence lens. It must not
imply that a 2018 pattern describes a 2026 team.

## Selected candidate — Corner War Room

### Manager fantasy and first-screen promise

Target user: a football fan who wants to put one corner-defending agreement on
trial against historical opponent evidence rather than browse another statistics
dashboard.

The first screen opens on a pitch with one yellow role token, dashed evidence
regions, and the memory line:

> **코너 수비에 한 명 더. 역습에는 한 명 덜.**
>
> **One more for the corner. One fewer for the break.**

The task immediately follows:

> `역습 1명`을 남길지, 코너 수비 임무로 돌릴지 선택하세요.
>
> Keep the yellow `1 for the break` role high, or move it to one defensive duty.

The visible caveat is: `결과 예측이 아닙니다. 2018년 브라질 코너 기록과
선택이 어디서 접촉하는지 비교합니다.`

The yellow role snaps to a mission slot at a region edge rather than a player
coordinate. The legend states `노란 표시는 정확한 선수 위치나 도달 범위가
아니라 추가 수비 임무입니다.` Every region carries `프로젝트 정의 구역 /
Project-defined region`.

The receipt opens only after the first move. Its section labels are fixed:

- `선택한 우선 구역과 겹친 브라질 기록`;
- `당시 수비팀 걷어내기·패스 기록 · 고정`.

The user sees the task before methodology or menus. After the user commits a
duty, one source-linked recorded window becomes available for explicit playback.
Nothing autoplays and the window is not called decisive.

### Evidence Council

This is a local, reproducible analyst workflow—not a runtime LLM chatbot.

- **관찰 (Scout):** reports the endpoint distribution, sample size, and boundary
  sensitivity.
- **반례 (Skeptic):** presents a shot window that remains outside the selected
  priority across every tested boundary and warns when the
  sample, region boundary, or definition is weak.
- **내 약속 (Coach):** translates the role move into one state receipt, such as
  `세컨드볼 대비 우선 · 역습 1명 수비 전환`, without recommending an optimal plan.

All three outputs are precomputed from versioned data. Every claim links to source
event identifiers. No API key, generated certainty, or private model is needed.

### Decision, manipulation, and consequence

- Decision: leave one outlet player high or bring that role back into one of four
  explicit priorities: check short option, near-post-side delivery lane,
  central-to-far delivery lane, or second-ball screen.
- Direct manipulation: drag the yellow outlet-role token between fixed duties; mobile
  and keyboard alternatives use `select → destination`.
- Visible consequence: step through recorded Brazil attacking evidence and mark
  only contact with the selected priority. In a separate, fixed column, show
  defending-team clearance/pass evidence whose straight-line event segment
  touched the project-defined outlet band. Never combine the two into a score or
  call it a successful release/counterattack.
- Tactical receipt: `observed attack evidence / manager role move / selected-region
  contact / outlet role brought back / assumption / least-explained window`.

Allowed claims are `recorded evidence touched this project-defined region`, `N of
M recorded windows touched the selected priority`, and `you prioritized X and
brought the outlet role back`.
The UI must not say that the setup would have stopped a goal, changed xG, won the
header, or produced a different result.

### Why it is not a formation board

The unit of play is a repeatable opponent problem and one role tradeoff, not
eleven markers on an empty pitch. The system surfaces the recorded window least
explained by the selected priority. The visual result is therefore an evidence
review, not a decorative tactic score.

### Real data and calculation

Primary candidate: Pappalardo and Massucco's Figshare Soccer Match Event Dataset,
whose Events item is marked CC BY 4.0 and includes spatio-temporal events for the
2018 FIFA World Cup. The source provides event time, type/subtype, player/team,
tags, and normalized start/end positions. It does not provide full-player tracking
or continuous ball trajectories.

The deterministic transform will:

1. select Brazil corner events and include both teams' events inside a fixed,
   documented 10-second window that is never labeled possession;
2. normalize all positions into Brazil's attacking frame and one corner side;
3. keep Brazil attacking primitives separate from defending-team outlet-band
   primitives and treat shot terminal placeholders as non-geometric;
4. use visible, project-defined priority regions without labeling them as learned
   tactical truth or real player reach;
5. compare Brazil endpoints/markers with the selected priority while displaying
   defending-team outlet-band evidence in a separate fixed column.

StatsBomb World Cup 2022 event/360 data remains research input only. Discovery
found 64 matches and 360 files for all 64, but its Public Data User Agreement
restricts raw-data reproduction/distribution and commercial use. It cannot power
the public prize entry without written permission covering this use and derived
publication.

FIFA's Enhanced Football Intelligence document is terminology context only. It
does not provide the underlying positions and does not validate our calculation.

### 60-second demo

1. `0–5s`: a claim-safe three-frame cold open shows the real initial role,
   `역습 1명 수비 전환`, and unexplained-shot/source-ID states under `코너 수비에
   한 명 더. 역습에는 한 명 덜.`
2. `5–12s`: the judge moves `역습 1명` to second-ball-screen duty.
3. `12–25s`: `내 약속` appears immediately after the move; the judge selects
   `재생` and one source-linked contact preset advances.
4. `25–32s`: selected Brazil evidence and fixed historical context appear under
   `두 기록은 서로 다른 장면이므로 하나의 점수로 합치지 않습니다`.
5. `32–38s`: the judge switches once to near-post duty and resets, proving a
   reversible real state transition without changing source evidence.
6. `38–55s`: the judge selects second-ball duty again, opens `반례 보기: 이
   선택으로 설명되지 않는 슈팅 기록`, and selects `재생`.
7. `55–60s`: the video holds on the non-contact verdict, source event ID, and
   2018/result-prediction boundary. The counterexample, not reset, is the final
   emotional frame.

### Smallest prototype and stop signal

Time box after the P0 media-statistics entry is frozen: one data spike plus one
interactive slice, capped at 12 focused hours. Scope is one opponent, all 42
audited corners, four visible defensive priorities, one movable role, three
replay windows, and one least-explained window.

Owner files:

- `scripts/derive-corner-scenarios.mjs`
- `public/data/corner-scenarios.json`
- `src/domain/cornerEvidence.ts`
- `src/domain/cornerEvidence.test.ts`
- `src/App.tsx`
- `tests/e2e/manager-loop.spec.ts`

Pass signals:

- four of five fresh viewers say within five seconds that adding one corner duty
  removes one attacking-transition role;
- three hand-calculated region-contact fixtures match the receipt exactly;
- a least-explained window or an honest `none found` state is always visible
  alongside the strongest endpoint pattern;
- drag, tap, and keyboard paths work without login, secret, or network calls;
- in a separate fresh delayed-recall cohort, at least three of five spontaneously
  recall the shot record the selection did not explain after three minutes; this
  cohort is required to pass the first-place differentiation claim, and without
  it comparison observations remain exploratory and the claim stays unproven;
- the data audit passes and attribution is visible in the product.

Stop or revise if the 42-row structural audit is not perfect, fewer than 38 rows
pass independent semantic review, a `±2` region-boundary sensitivity change
reverses the headline or primary tradeoff, short corners are not first-class,
outlet-band evidence cannot remain defending-team-only, two of five users mistake
contact for a stopped attack or the fixed column for a simulated result, or the
license/attribution chain is incomplete.

## Runner-up — Eight-Second Coverage Rehearsal

Manager fantasy: rewind a decisive World Cup attack to an event snapshot, assign
one defender a different cover target, and see the recorded event path it overlaps
and the space it leaves.

- Strength: the most emotional first screen and 60-second demo.
- Guardrail: it is a cover-target rehearsal, never a claim that the goal was
  prevented or that players were continuously tracked.
- Data need: event-linked positions and visible-area context; StatsBomb 360 is an
  excellent technical fit but not cleared for this public prize use.
- Minimum slice: one scene, one cover target inside an explicit reach envelope,
  event-by-event steps, one receipt, one counterexample.
- Stop signal: reject if permission is unavailable, the key space is outside the
  visible polygon, the result changes under a small reach-radius sensitivity, or
  two of five viewers think they simulated a saved goal.

## Current-data challenger — 2026 Press Pact

Manager fantasy: after a real 2026 match, receive a rematch and make one pressing
promise—line height, compactness, and whether to show inside or outside—then see
the observed evidence that the promise explains and the escape it must concede.

- Data: three rights-cleared FIFA 2026 reports for the same team/opponent, limited
  to report-level formations, phase shares, observed average geometry,
  through/around/over line-break mix, and pressure-direction totals.
- Manipulation: snap a block line to high/mid/low, adjust team length, and choose
  show-inside/show-outside. Freehand trap drawing is rejected because report
  aggregates provide no arbitrary-zone coordinates.
- Consequence: compare the instruction with observed average shapes and spotlight
  a related route risk. Published bars never change in response to the user's
  move; manager instruction and observed evidence remain visually separate.
- Memory line: **Draw the press. Admit the escape.** / **압박을 그려라. 열어둔
  탈출로를 인정하라.**
- Risk: FIFA permission is not granted; through/around/over are line-break methods,
  not left/centre/right channels; aggregates cannot show that a trap succeeds.
- Stop signal: no written prize-use permission, fewer than three prior reports,
  extraction agreement below 99% across 30 audited cells, or any UI claim that a
  user instruction changed turnovers, xG, or the match result.

| Originality | Data credibility | Interaction | Visual clarity | Reliability | Finishability |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 5 | 2 | 4 | 5 | 3 | 3 |

Gate: `REVISE`. It may replace Corner War Room only with written permission,
three-report reproducibility, an honest plan/evidence separation, and a passing
five-second comprehension test. Until then, borrow only its current-match framing
and "admit the escape" tradeoff language.

## Scope boundary

This was the one bounded P1 exploration lane while the media-statistics
competition remained P0. The historical transform/test gate now passes and the
planning PDF exists. The current boundary is still the portfolio board: preserve
the verified product and do not start external release work or feature expansion
before the P0 freeze. No report metric enters the product before approval.

## 2026-07-18 research and UX lock

The research and professional-persona review in
`docs/research-ux-review-2026-07-18.md` strengthens the conditional selection but
did not by itself promote the product. The replacement implementation now exists;
three decisions remain locked:

1. Use a pitch-centred `선택 -> 기록 -> 반례 -> 재선택/초기화` flow. Choice,
   context, and explanation remain in the same stage rather than separate score
   cards.
2. Treat the yellow object as a tactical duty contract, never an inferred player
   coordinate, learned marking assignment, or reach envelope.
3. Borrow research interaction patterns without borrowing validation. The app
   does not reproduce TacticAI, predict an alternative result, or optimise a
   corner. Rule-based historical retrieval is labeled as such.

The former Touchline Lab implementation remains historical `REJECT` and has been
replaced, not restyled. Corner War Room has passed its `320x568` geometry,
78/78 unit/contract suite, and 56/56 pre-release gates across Chromium, mobile
Chromium, Firefox, and WebKit, including direct manipulation, replay,
counterexample, reset, Axe, forced colours, and reduced motion. BG-12 remains
pending for the stamped public release. Physical VoiceOver and human
comprehension are unavailable and support no usability, preference, or recall
claim. Apple-style means purpose, hierarchy, agency, and restraint; it does not
mean decorative glass surfaces.
